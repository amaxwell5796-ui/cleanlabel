The Problem

What food labels don't tell you

Heavy metals like arsenic, cadmium and lead enter food through contaminated soil and water. They're invisible, odourless, and absent from nutritional labels — yet they accumulate in the body over time.

🍼

Baby Food & Weaning

Baby foods using rice, root vegetables and leafy greens carry elevated risk. Infants' smaller body weight means even low-level exposure can exceed safe limits.

Arsenic found at 6× the infant safe limit in some UK baby foods
🍫

Chocolate & Cocoa

Cocoa plants are particularly efficient at absorbing cadmium from soil. Dark chocolate — often marketed as a health food — can contain cadmium at concerning levels.

Cadmium detected in 70%+ of dark chocolate products tested
💊

Supplements & Protein Powders

The UK supplement industry is poorly regulated for heavy metal contamination. Plant-based protein powders can contain elevated arsenic and lead.

Lead found above safe limits in 1 in 3 protein powders tested
🌿

Herbs, Spices & Rice

Dried herbs and spices concentrate heavy metals during processing. Brown rice absorbs arsenic at a significantly higher rate than almost any other crop.

1 in 3 common spices contains heavy metals at concerning levels
